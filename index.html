CTBC: Contact-Triggered Blind Climbing for Wheeled Bipedal Robots with Instruction Learning and Reinforcement Learning

Abstract

In recent years, wheeled bipedal robots have gained increasing attention due to their advantages in mobility, such as high-speed locomotion on flat terrain. However, their performance on complex environments (e.g., staircases) remains inferior to that of traditional legged robots. To address this limitation, we propose a contact-triggered blind climbing (CTBC) method for wheeled bipedal robots. Upon detecting wheel-obstacle contact, the robot triggers a leg-lifting motion to overcome the obstacle. By incorporating a feedforward trajectory, our method ensures timely leg-lifting actions, significantly improving the robot’s ability to traverse unstructured terrains. The approach has been experimentally validated and successfully deployed on LimX Dynamics' wheeled bipedal robot, Tron1. Real-world tests demonstrate that Tron1 can reliably climb obstacles up to 20 cm in height using only proprioceptive feedback.
